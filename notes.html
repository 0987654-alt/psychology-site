Unit 1: Biological Bases of Behavior

Neurons and Neural Transmission

  • Neuron: Basic building block of the nervous system
  • Action Potential: Electrical signal that travels down the axon
  • Synapse: Gap between neurons where neurotransmitters are released
  • Myelin Sheath: Fatty tissue that speeds up neural transmission

Brain Structure and Function

  • Brainstem: Controls vital functions (breathing, heart rate)
  • Cerebellum: Balance, coordination, motor learning
  • Limbic System: Emotion, memory, motivation
  • Amygdala: Fear, aggression, emotional processing
  • Hippocampus: Memory formation and spatial navigation
  • Hypothalamus: Regulates hormones, body temperature, hunger
  • Thalamus: Relay station for sensory information
  • Cerebral Cortex: Higher-order thinking, consciousness

Neurotransmitters

  • Dopamine: Reward, motivation, movement disorders
  • Serotonin: Mood regulation, sleep, appetite
  • GABA: Inhibitory neurotransmitter, reduces anxiety
  • Acetylcholine: Muscle movement, memory, attention
  • Norepinephrine: Alertness, arousal, fight-or-flight

Unit 2: Sensation and Perception

Vision

  • Cornea: Clear covering that bends light
  • Pupil: Opening that controls light entry
  • Lens: Focuses light on the retina
  • Retina: Contains photoreceptors (rods and cones)
  • Rods: Detect light/dark, peripheral vision
  • Cones: Detect color, central vision
  • Blind Spot: Where optic nerve exits retina

Hearing

  • Frequency: Determines pitch
  • Amplitude: Determines loudness
  • Cochlea: Fluid-filled structure that converts sound waves
  • Hair Cells: Receptors that detect sound vibrations

Perceptual Principles

  • Gestalt Principles: How we organize visual information
  • Figure-Ground: Distinguishing objects from background
  • Proximity: Objects close together are perceived as groups
  • Similarity: Similar objects are grouped together
  • Closure: We complete incomplete figures mentally

Unit 3: Learning

Classical Conditioning

Ivan Pavlov: Learning through association ‚Äî pairing a neutral stimulus with a reflex.

  • Unconditioned Stimulus (UCS): Naturally triggers response
  • Unconditioned Response (UCR): Natural response to UCS
  • Conditioned Stimulus (CS): Previously neutral stimulus
  • Conditioned Response (CR): Learned response to CS
  • Acquisition: Initial learning phase
  • Extinction: Weakening of CR when CS presented alone
  • Spontaneous Recovery: Reappearance of CR after rest
  • Generalization: Responding to similar stimuli
  • Discrimination: Responding only to specific stimulus

Operant Conditioning

B.F. Skinner: Behavior is influenced by consequences (reinforcements or punishments).

  • Positive Reinforcement: Adding pleasant stimulus
  • Negative Reinforcement: Removing unpleasant stimulus
  • Positive Punishment: Adding unpleasant stimulus
  • Negative Punishment: Removing pleasant stimulus
  • Continuous Reinforcement: Every response reinforced
  • Partial Reinforcement: Some responses reinforced
  • Fixed Ratio: Reinforcement after set number of responses
  • Variable Ratio: Reinforcement after varying responses
  • Fixed Interval: Reinforcement after set time period
  • Variable Interval: Reinforcement after varying time

Observational Learning

  • Albert Bandura: Learning by watching others
  • Modeling: Imitating observed behavior
  • Vicarious Learning: Learning from others' consequences

Unit 4: Cognitive Psychology

Memory

  • Encoding: Getting information into memory
  • Storage: Retaining information over time
  • Retrieval: Getting information out of memory
  • Sensory Memory: Brief storage of sensory information
  • Short-term Memory: Limited capacity, 15-30 seconds
  • Working Memory: Active processing of information
  • Long-term Memory: Unlimited capacity, permanent storage
  • Explicit Memory: Conscious recall (episodic, semantic)
  • Implicit Memory: Unconscious memory (procedural, priming)

Thinking and Problem Solving

  • Algorithm: Step-by-step problem-solving procedure
  • Heuristic: Mental shortcut or rule of thumb
  • Availability Heuristic: Judging by ease of recall
  • Representative Heuristic: Judging by similarity to prototype
  • Confirmation Bias: Seeking information that confirms beliefs
  • Functional Fixedness: Inability to see new uses for objects

Language

  • Phonemes: Smallest units of sound
  • Morphemes: Smallest units of meaning
  • Syntax: Rules for combining words
  • Semantics: Meaning of words and sentences
  • Chomsky: Language Acquisition Device (LAD)
  • Critical Period: Optimal time for language learning

Unit 5: Developmental Psychology

Piaget's Cognitive Development

  • Sensorimotor Stage (0-2): Learning through senses and motor actions
  • Preoperational Stage (2-7): Symbolic thinking, egocentrism
  • Concrete Operational (7-11): Logical thinking about concrete objects
  • Formal Operational (11+): Abstract and hypothetical thinking
  • Schema: Mental framework for understanding
  • Assimilation: Fitting new info into existing schemas
  • Accommodation: Changing schemas to fit new info

Erikson's Psychosocial Development

  • Trust vs. Mistrust (0-1): Basic trust in caregivers
  • Autonomy vs. Shame (1-3): Developing independence
  • Initiative vs. Guilt (3-5): Taking on new challenges
  • Industry vs. Inferiority (5-12): Developing competence
  • Identity vs. Role Confusion (12-18): Developing sense of self
  • Intimacy vs. Isolation (18-40): Forming relationships
  • Generativity vs. Stagnation (40-65): Contributing to society
  • Integrity vs. Despair (65+): Reflecting on life

Attachment Theory

  • Secure Attachment: Comfortable with closeness and independence
  • Anxious-Ambivalent: Clingy, anxious about relationships
  • Avoidant: Uncomfortable with closeness
  • Disorganized: Inconsistent attachment patterns

Unit 6: Personality

Freud's Psychoanalytic Theory

  • Id: Pleasure principle, unconscious desires
  • Ego: Reality principle, mediates id and superego
  • Superego: Moral principle, conscience
  • Defense Mechanisms: Unconscious strategies to reduce anxiety
  • Repression: Pushing threatening thoughts into unconscious
  • Projection: Attributing own feelings to others
  • Displacement: Redirecting emotions to safer target

Trait Theories

  • Big Five (OCEAN):
  • Openness: Creativity, imagination, openness to experience
  • Conscientiousness: Organization, responsibility, self-discipline
  • Extraversion: Sociability, assertiveness, positive emotions
  • Agreeableness: Cooperation, trust, empathy
  • Neuroticism: Emotional instability, anxiety, moodiness

Humanistic Theory

  • Carl Rogers: Self-concept and unconditional positive regard
  • Abraham Maslow: Hierarchy of needs, self-actualization
  • Self-efficacy: Belief in one's ability to succeed

Unit 7: Abnormal Psychology

Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder: Persistent, excessive worry
  • Panic Disorder: Recurrent panic attacks
  • Phobias: Irrational fear of specific objects/situations
  • OCD: Obsessive thoughts and compulsive behaviors
  • PTSD: Following exposure to traumatic events

Mood Disorders

  • Major Depression: Persistent sadness, loss of interest
  • Bipolar Disorder: Alternating manic and depressive episodes
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder: Depression during specific seasons

Schizophrenia

  • Positive Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech
  • Negative Symptoms: Flat affect, social withdrawal, lack of motivation
  • Dopamine Hypothesis: Excess dopamine activity in brain

Personality Disorders

  • Antisocial: Disregard for others' rights
  • Borderline: Unstable relationships and self-image
  • Narcissistic: Grandiose sense of self-importance

Unit 8: Treatment of Abnormal Behavior

Psychotherapy Approaches

  • Psychoanalysis: Uncovering unconscious conflicts
  • Cognitive Therapy: Changing negative thought patterns
  • Behavioral Therapy: Changing maladaptive behaviors
  • Cognitive-Behavioral (CBT): Combining cognitive and behavioral
  • Humanistic Therapy: Client-centered, unconditional positive regard
  • Group Therapy: Treatment in group setting
  • Family Therapy: Treating family system

Biomedical Treatments

  • Antidepressants: SSRIs, increase serotonin availability
  • Antianxiety: Benzodiazepines, enhance GABA activity
  • Antipsychotics: Block dopamine receptors
  • Mood Stabilizers: Lithium for bipolar disorder
  • ECT: Electroconvulsive therapy for severe depression

Unit 9: Social Psychology

Social Influence

  • Conformity: Changing behavior to match group
  • Asch Conformity: Line length study showing group pressure
  • Obedience: Following orders from authority
  • Milgram Study: Shocking learners, 65% obeyed completely
  • Social Facilitation: Performance improves with audience
  • Social Loafing: Reduced effort in group settings
  • Groupthink: Faulty decision-making in cohesive groups

Attribution Theory

  • Internal Attribution: Behavior due to personality/character
  • External Attribution: Behavior due to situation
  • Fundamental Attribution Error: Overestimating internal factors
  • Self-serving Bias: Attributing success to internal, failure to external

Prejudice and Discrimination

  • Prejudice: Negative attitude toward group
  • Discrimination: Negative behavior toward group
  • Stereotype: Overgeneralized belief about group
  • In-group Bias: Favoring one's own group
  • Contact Hypothesis: Interaction reduces prejudice

Aggression and Helping

  • Frustration-Aggression: Blocked goals lead to aggression
  • Social Learning: Aggression learned through observation
  • Bystander Effect: Less likely to help with more people present
  • Diffusion of Responsibility: Responsibility spread among group
  • Altruism: Helping others without expectation of reward
